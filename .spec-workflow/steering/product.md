# Product Overview

## Product Purpose

CYOA (working title: **The Unwritten**) is a browser- and mobile-playable choose-your-own-adventure game whose stories are generated by an LLM in real time. Existing CYOA tools force authors to hand-craft every node; existing AI fiction tools wander without consequence. CYOA closes that gap by pairing AI-authored prose with a deterministic game engine — vitality, currency, inventory, hidden stats, branching memory, and unlockable endings — so player agency has weight without anyone having to pre-write the tree.

The interface aspires to feel like a "living book": gothic, candlelit, paged. The product hides the prompt-engineering scaffolding under the metaphor of a tome that writes itself in response to the reader.

## Target Users

- **Story-first readers** — people who want interactive fiction that reads like literature rather than a stat-grind. They care about prose, atmosphere, and meaningful choice over mechanics.
- **Light RPG fans** — players comfortable with vitality/inventory/permadeath who want emergent stories without a Game Master.
- **Casual mobile players** — anyone who wants 5–15 minute reading sessions on a phone, picked up and put down across the day.
- **Co-op groups** — pairs/small groups who want a "campfire" experience around one device, taking turns choosing.

Pain points: existing AI chat fiction has no consequences, no save state, and no shared visual language; existing CYOA games (Twine, Choice of Games) are fully scripted, so replays expose the seams.

## Key Features

These features are anchored to the **Game Designer's Specification: CYOA Web Engine** (the "Game Spec"), which is the authoritative source of truth for engine behavior. Section references below point back to it.

1. **Narrative branching & logic** *(Game Spec §2.A)* — every scene leads to ≥1 choice or a Game Over / The End. Choices are conditional on items and stats (hidden or shown-as-locked, designer's call). Invisible **global flags / story tags** (`met_queen`, `betrayed_thieves`) let later scenes change in response to earlier actions.
2. **Player attribute system** *(Game Spec §2.B)* — **Vitality** (Health; 0 → Death scene), **Currency** (Gold), **Inventory** (unique items, usable as keys to unlock branches), **Hidden Stats** (Morality, Infamy, etc. — invisible to player, affect NPC reactions and prose).
3. **Scene-level effects** *(Game Spec §2.C)* — **Auto-Modifiers** that fire on entering a node ("Poisoned Room → −5 Health") and **Delayed Consequences** (set a flag that fires N nodes later).
4. **AI-generated narrative with persistent memory** — scenes are composed live by an LLM that receives the full game state (stats, inventory, flags, prior choices) so prose is consistent with the world it's playing in. The LLM is wrapped by the deterministic engine, not the other way around.
5. **Curated starter adventures** — a small library of designer-seeded tales, including the **"Escape the Training Room"** tutorial, which teaches the choice / consequence / stat loop in 3 rooms.
6. **Story Mode and Hardcore Mode** *(Game Spec §3)* — Story has Undo/Rewind (one step) and Bookmarks; Hardcore has no rewind and **permadeath** (save purged). Toggleable mid-game with caveats (downgrade always OK; upgrade disables existing save's hardcore-only ending unlocks).
7. **Atmospheric themes & typography** *(Game Spec §4)* — **Day / Night / Sepia** themes; **Sans-Serif / Serif** (and Mono) toggle; text-size slider; full-width background images and ambient sound loops on key scenes (Pro).
8. **Dynamic stat feedback** *(Game Spec §4)* — every stat change emits a brief animated indicator at the moment it happens (`+10 Gold`, `♥ −5`); never silent, never delayed. Four stats-visibility modes (persistent / peek-drawer / contextual / on-demand sheet) let the player tune how loud the HUD is.
9. **Modular content + reusable Common Result nodes** *(Game Spec §5)* — stories are written as discrete nodes; multiple choices from different scenes can point to a single Common Result node to keep the graph manageable.
10. **Endings tracking** *(Game Spec §5)* — a branching map of "what-might-have-been" plus a trophy crypt of unlocked endings across all playthroughs, shareable.
11. **Co-op — pass-the-controller and remote rooms** — multiple readers, one tale. Day-one: pass-the-controller (one device, rotate per choice / per scene / vote-to-decide). Day-one: shareable **remote room URL** so friends on different devices read and choose together in real time (Convex's reactive backbone makes this cheap to ship from the start). No accounts required for secondary readers.
12. **Publish & share your tale** — at any point during or after a run, the player can publish the tale they've experienced (the actual generated prose, choices, stat trajectory) as a shareable URL. Friends can:
    - **Read along** in a paged, read-only view that mirrors the player's exact run.
    - **Fork from any decision** to seed a new run of their own from that branch (uses the same engine state at the fork point + a fresh LLM continuation).
    - See the **shared cover, title, and one-line synopsis** the publisher can edit.
    Privacy modes: public, unlisted (link-only), friends-only (account list). Published tales are first-class marketing artifacts and seed the discovery library.
13. **Designer / creator authoring** — players and designers can publish **seeded adventures** (a hand-crafted opening, tone, and rule overrides) that other players can launch as their own runs with AI continuation. Creator earns a share of subscription revenue tied to play-time on their adventures.
14. **Ambient soundscape (Pro)** — per-scene-tagged ambient audio loops (cathedral hum, distant rain, candle crackle), synced to scene tags and theme. Mutable; respects system mute and `prefers-reduced-motion`-equivalent audio preferences.
15. **Achievements & seasons** — global, time-limited tales with shared ending leaderboards ("first to find," "rarest path"). Drives recurring engagement beyond the daily-turn loop and gives published tales a competitive surface.
16. **Free-to-play with daily turn limit, subscription unlock, Pro tier** — guests get a daily allotment of turns; subscription removes the cap; Pro adds AI-generated scene **illustrations** (Gemini), short **scene-cinematic videos** (Veo) at key story beats, and the ambient soundscape pack.
17. **Stripe-first monetization with native purchase support** — Stripe is the primary subscription, invoice, customer portal, credit, usage-meter, upgrade, downgrade, cancellation, and tax-record system. iOS App Store and Google Play purchases are supported where app-store policy requires them, then normalized into the same Convex entitlement model so a player who subscribes on one surface gets the same Unlimited/Pro state everywhere.
18. **Web + native, one codebase** — built so the same UI ships to the browser, iOS, and Android from day one via Expo. Native enables system-level features like background ambient audio, push notifications when the daily candle re-lights, and lock-screen quick-resume.

## Business Objectives

- Convert anonymous landings into engaged sessions without forcing signup (guest-first).
- Convert engaged guests into account holders by anchoring save state to the account ("save your tale").
- **Drive primary signup through Stripe-backed account billing**, with native apps as acquisition and engagement surfaces and native purchase flows available where store policy requires them.
- Convert account holders into Unlimited subscribers when daily turn caps bite — same subscription state across native and web.
- Convert subscribers into Pro subscribers via in-context illustration / video upsells.
- Build a moat through the **endings tracking** mechanic and **published tales** — the more endings unlocked and tales published / shared with friends, the higher the switching cost.

## Success Metrics

- **Activation**: % of new sessions that complete ≥5 turns of any story.
- **Tutorial completion**: % of first-time guests who finish "Escape the Training Room."
- **Guest → account**: % of guests who hit the soft-signup prompt and convert (target prompt at turn 3).
- **Free → paid**: % of accounts that subscribe within 7 days of first hitting the daily-turn paywall.
- **Paid → Pro**: % of subscribers who upgrade to Pro within 30 days.
- **Channel mix**: % of new subscriptions originating from Stripe web checkout vs. iOS IAP vs. Google Play IAP. Target: Stripe is the default paid path while native purchases remain functional and entitlement-equivalent.
- **Cross-platform subscription continuity**: % of subscribers who play on ≥2 surfaces in any 30-day window — proxy for the unified-account promise being felt.
- **Retention**: D1 / D7 / D30 by cohort and by signup channel.
- **Endings unlocked per active account** — proxy for emotional investment.
- **Tales published per active account** + **average reads per published tale** — virality and creator-loop health.
- **Co-op session rate** — % of sessions with 2+ readers (pass-the-controller or remote room).

## Product Principles

1. **Story first, mechanics second.** The book metaphor is the brand. Every UI decision should preserve the feeling of reading a tome that's responding to you. Stats and HUDs hide unless asked.
2. **No friction before the hook.** Guests can read immediately. Signup, mode-pick, and character creation are deferred until the player has a reason to care.
3. **Agency is the point.** A flavor-only choice is an anti-feature. Every choice should change a stat, a flag, an item, or an ending — even if subtly. **Game Spec §6 rule applies: no more than three flavor choices in a row.**
4. **Consequences are immediate and felt.** Per Game Spec §4 and §6, stat changes animate at the moment they happen, never buried in a sheet. Death is brutal and full-screen.
5. **Story → inventory navigation must feel instantaneous.** Game Spec §6 (Flow). Inventory is one tap away from any reading view; no loading state, no route change perceptible to the player.
6. **One codebase, two surfaces.** Web and native share the same component tree. If a feature can't be built once and shipped to both, it gets re-scoped.
7. **Pay for joy, not for entry.** Free players get a complete, beatable experience daily. Subscriptions sell *more*; Pro sells *richer*. Never paywall a save mid-scene without a warning.
8. **The book closes when the candle gutters.** Daily turn caps are a narrative event, not an error message — the metaphor is preserved even at the paywall.

## Monitoring & Visibility

- **Player-facing dashboard**: Endings Map (the branching web of paths taken) and Trophy Crypt (grid of unlocked endings). Per-account, persistent across runs.
- **Operator-facing dashboard** (in-house, no third-party trackers): cohort funnel for activation → tutorial → signup → paid → Pro; AI cost per session (per provider); daily active reads; co-op adoption. Built in the same React tree as the player app, gated to admin accounts.
- **Real-time stat feedback in-game**: every stat change emits an animated pip (`♥ −5`, `+10 gold`) over the prose; pip fades after ~3s but is also recorded in the character sheet.
- **Sharing**: per-ending share links ("first to find" cinematic death screen variant), branching-map screenshots, account-scoped trophy room URLs.

## Future Vision

CYOA grows from a single AI-CYOA reader into a **library + studio + social layer for AI-native interactive fiction**: a place where readers, creators, and friends share one identity, one engine, and one ending-tracking surface across solo runs, co-op rooms, published tales, and creator-authored seeds.

The longer-horizon bets — beyond the day-one feature set above — are about depth, not new product surfaces:

- **Per-tale heatmap analytics for creators**: where readers die, branch, and quit on each published / authored tale, feeding back into seed curation and creator earnings dashboards.
- **Cross-tale narrative continuity**: optional shared-world settings where stat / flag state from one ending carries into a later tale — the "tome that remembers across volumes."
- **Voice and audiobook mode**: TTS narration of the generated prose with per-scene ambient mix; offline cache for transit reading on native.
- **Designer revenue share dashboard**: creators see live earnings from their authored seeds; payout via Stripe Connect.
- **Localized models**: route per-locale tales to language-native models for prose quality.
